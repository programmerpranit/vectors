RC Racing

Team Description:-

A maximum of 6 team members are allowed in a team. A team can participatewith a maximum of 2 cars. All cars will have a different driver and once the driver name has beenfixed at the start of the event it cannot be changed during the whole event. Teams from different educational institutes can participate.

Car Dimensions:-

The car should fit in a box of dimensions 700mm x 500mm x 600mm throughout the race, excluding the external device which is used to control the car.

Gameplay

Each team will get Two trials. Each trial consists of Three laps. The fastest trial will be considered. Only one member will be allowed in the arena at a time. Checkpoints will be placed throughout the arena. If a machine is stuck or falls off the arena, that team will be allowed to pick it up and place it at a checkpoint behind that point, the time for which will be added to the total lap time. If any of the cars starts off before the flag is waved, the counter would be restarted and the car will get a second chance. However, if any car starts off before the waving of flag (or countdown) for a second time, it will be disqualified. Once disqualified the team can't participate again in the event.

Rules and Regulations:-

1. The teams must design a purely wireless remote-controlled machine.
2. The machine parts can be roughly classified into structural and functional parts: Functional parts - ready-made versions of Gears, differential gear, engine, springs, shock absorbers, servo motors (non-propulsion purposes only), batteries and wheels can be used. Structural parts - Chassis, steering mechanism, shock towers and suspension (excluding the upper suspension arm, suspension spring and shock absorbers) have to be built by the participants themselves.
3. Judging for the same will be strict and the participant will be immediately disqualified if any of the above structural components are found to be readymade.
4. Minimum thickness of tires is 3 inch. Selection of a good width will ensure better performance in dirt tracks.
5. Brake Mechanism: It is compulsory to incorporate a braking mechanism in the car. Participants have to fabricate the brake pad as a part of the braking mechanism. Any other part used in braking mechanism (including the brake disk) can be readymade.
6. Wheel Hub: Any part rigidly attached to the wheel hub will be considered as a part of it and hence can be ready-made. E.g:- ball stud.
7. Steering Mechanism: Any part which is connected to steering rod rigidly i.e. has no degrees of freedom with respect to steering rod will be considered as part of steering rod and thus has to be fabricated by participants.
8. A team is disqualified if it takes any of the following actions during the gameplay:-
- The team intentionally damages or tries to damage the field, facilities, equipment or opponent’s car.
- The team performs any acts that are not in the spirit of fair play.
- The team fails to obey instructions or warning issued by referees.
- If a car disintegrates in the arena leaving behind loose parts the team will be disqualified.
- Once the race begins team members will be immediately disqualified if found touching their or opponent’s machine
- The organizers reserve the right to change the rules and regulations of the competition at any time. All contestants are expected to comply with any changes made by the organizers.
- Prize money might change depending on entries. T&C applied
